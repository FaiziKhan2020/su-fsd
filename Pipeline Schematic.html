§ 01

Stage by stage

Each row: who, what they do, what comes out, and what has to clear to advance.
STAGE 01

Idea

Captured concept; needs a champion.
Typical age 1–7 days
Surface Idea Inbox (rail)
Inputs
  • Trend signal — TikTok, Reddit, Amazon search rising
  • Customer ask — review/support patterns
  • Adjacent SKU gap — "we sell X, should sell Y"
  • Supplier cold-call — "we have a new ingredient…"
Who
  • Chesky Triage (single decider)
  • Anyone Submit ideas
What happens
  • Idea enters the Inbox with a one-line pitch
  • Chesky decides: promote to research, reject with reason, or park
Outputs
  • One-liner pitch
  • Brand bucket (PN or DON)
  • Product type guess (liquid / capsule / powder)
  • Promote → assigns to Malik
Gate to advance Chesky clicks Promote. Idea becomes a Product, Malik is auto-owner, stage flips to Niche Research.
STAGE 02

Niche Research

Malik writes the deep dive — market, competitors, ingredients.
Typical duration 5–10 days
Artifact Niche Doc (Google Doc)
Inputs
  • Promoted idea + Chesky's brief
  • Helium 10 / Jungle Scout pulls
  • Competitor ASIN review
  • Ingredient research (PubMed, examine.com)
Who
  • Malik Writes the doc
What happens
  • Malik fills 6-tab template: market, competitors, ingredients, claims, positioning, financials
  • System parses doc into structured fields when uploaded
  • "Researching" → "Parsed" sub-state
Outputs
  • Niche analysis doc — 6 tabs filled
  • Recommended formula (Malik + PDS)
  • Target retail price + COGS budget
  • 3-5 competitor ASINs to beat
Gate to advance Malik uploads the parsed doc. Stage flips to Niche Review and the dual-approval queue lights up for Chesky + Joel.
STAGE 03

Niche Review

Two-of-two approval. Chesky decides product. Joel decides money.
Typical duration 2–5 days
Artifact Approval record (per approver)
Inputs
  • Parsed niche doc
  • Comments thread on the product
  • Margin model + COGS estimate
Who
  • Chesky Strategic fit
  • Joel Margin / capital
What happens
  • Each independently: Approve, Request changes, or Reject
  • Mobile-friendly — approvable from phone
  • Both must approve before advance
Outputs
  • Both approvals stamped + dated
  • Or — kill with category + reason
  • Or — sent back for niche revisions
Gate to advance Both Chesky AND Joel = ✓. Auto-advances to R&D and routes by product type (see matrix below).
STAGE 04

R&D / Sampling

Formula gets real. Samples ship. Iterations until "approved."
Typical duration 3–8 weeks
Artifact Final spec sheet
Inputs
  • Approved niche doc + recommended formula
  • Manufacturer auto-locked (by type)
  • Ronel's ingredient supplier rolodex
Who
  • Talha Liquid / powder formulator
  • Mimi · Connor Capsule reviewers
  • Ronel Ships ingredient samples
  • Malik Owns the gate
What happens
  • Ronel ships ingredient samples to Talha (or to mfg)
  • Talha iterates — flavor, blend, stability
  • Verdicts: good, tweak, reject
  • Loop until everyone signs off
Outputs
  • Final formula locked (mg per ingredient, flavor, carrier)
  • Spec sheet — what mfg will execute
  • Approved physical sample (we keep one)
Gate to advance Sample approved by formula owner + Malik. Spec sheet finalized. Stage flips to In Build — three streams kick off in parallel.
STAGE 05

In Build

Three streams run in parallel — sourcing, design, listing. Detailed below.
Typical duration 3–6 weeks
Artifacts BOM · Mocks · Listing draft
Inputs
  • Final spec sheet
  • Locked manufacturer + production run size
  • Brand guidelines (PN or DON)
Who (parallel)
  • Ronel Sourcing stream
  • Esty Design stream
  • April Listing stream
What happens
  • Streams run independently — own owners, own gates
  • Each stream's progress shows on the card as 3 dots
  • Comments + AI nudges keep streams in sync
Outputs
  • Sourcing → BOM with picks + ordered status
  • Design → approved label + box artwork
  • Listing → main image + bullets + A+ assets
Gate to advance ALL THREE streams complete. POs placed (outside system). Mfg confirms start date. Stage flips to In Production.
STAGE 06

In Production

Mfg makes units. We track the buy and prep launch.
Typical duration 4–10 weeks
Artifact Production Buy panel
Inputs
  • Confirmed BOM + locked mfg
  • Run size (units)
  • Approved artwork files for printer
Who
  • Ronel Tracks BOM order status
  • Mfg Manufactures + bottles
  • April Stages listing for launch
What happens
  • Production Buy panel shows ordered/not-ordered per line
  • Critical-lead alerts when an unordered item blocks the run
  • Sole-source vendors flagged 🔒
Outputs
  • Finished units arrive at FBA / 3PL
  • Lot codes + expiry dated
  • Listing ready to flip live
Gate to advance Units received + listing approved. April flips it live. Stage flips to Launched.
STAGE 07

Launched

Live on Amazon. Thesis vs. actual outcome tracked.
Window 90-day post-launch review
Artifact Live tab — outcome card
Inputs
  • Niche thesis (sales target, BSR target)
  • Actual sales feed
  • Reviews + customer signals
Who
  • April Listing perf + reviews
  • Joel Margin reality check
  • Chesky Reorder / iterate / sunset
What happens
  • Thesis vs. actual delta visible on Live tab
  • If >50% under thesis at 90d → flag for retro
  • Reorder triggers based on sell-through
Outputs
  • Sell-through curve
  • Margin actuals
  • Reorder decision
  • Lessons → fed back into Niche template
Loop closed Outcomes inform the next round of niche research. The system learns; the team doesn't repeat mistakes.

§ 04

Off-ramps & loops

Not every idea makes it through. Here's where products exit, pause, or loop back.

Killed

From: Idea · Niche Review · R&D

Hard no. Reason captured (margin, market saturation, regulatory, supply). Stays searchable in killed bucket so the team can spot patterns and not re-pitch the same dead idea in 6 months.

On Hold

From: Any active stage

Soft pause. Waiting on something external — supply availability, a competitor move, a brand decision. Surfaces in the rail with a "revisit on" date. Auto-pings owner when that hits.

Back to Niche

From: Niche Review

"Request changes" sends it back to Malik with specific notes. Doesn't burn the product — keeps the same ID, comments, history. Just unlocks the doc for revisions.
