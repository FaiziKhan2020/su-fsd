§ 02a

Open question: Where does the Niche Analysis live?

Two viable directions. Same content, different tool.

Option A · Keep Google Sheets, parse on upload

Status quo, AI-augmented

Malik keeps using the existing 6-tab sheet (familiar; collaborative; great for tables, formulas, cross-tab refs). Drops the file into the system. Opus 4.7 parses it into structured fields and a clean reviewer-facing summary. Reviewers see the summary by default, the raw sheet one click away.

Pros
Zero migration. Malik keeps his flow. Sheets handle math + tables natively.
Cons
Two systems to keep in sync. Parser drift if Malik changes the template. Harder to enforce required fields.

Option B · Native template inside the system

Migrate, then enforce

Same 6 sections, but as in-system structured forms with required fields, ingredient picker connected to Ronel's vendor DB, competitor ASIN puller, etc. No parsing because data is structured at entry. Wireframe upload is first-class.

Pros
Single source of truth. Required-field enforcement. Data flows directly into PDS, BOM, listing.
Cons
Migration cost. Malik has to learn a new tool. Loses Sheets' tabular flexibility for messy research notes.
STAGE 03

Cost Pass

Quick ingredient $/bottle from Ronel before approvers see it. Informational, not blocking.
Typical duration 1–2 days
Artifact Cost panel on the dossier
Scope Ingredients / actives only
Inputs
  • Parsed Niche Analysis (recommended formula + dose ranges)
  • Ronel's vendor rolodex + recent quotes
  • Run-size assumption (typical MOQ)
Who
  • Ronel Runs the pass
What happens
  • Ronel pulls quick numbers on the proposed actives
  • Capsule, blending, fill costs are constants — auto-filled from product type, no quoting needed
  • Output: a single $/bottle figure for ingredients with a one-line note
Outputs
  • $/bottle on actives — landed estimate
  • Flag if any active looks unreasonable ("Ashwa KSM-66 at this dose blows the budget")
  • Cost panel rendered next to the approve button
Gate to advance Ronel marks the cost pass complete. Informational only — it doesn't block Niche Review, but Joel + Chesky now see the number. Auto-advances to Niche Review.
STAGE 04

Niche Review

Joel + Chesky bind the decision. Malik recommends, Ronel's cost panel sits alongside.
Typical duration 2–5 days
Artifact Approval record (per approver)
Inputs
  • Parsed Niche Analysis (Opus 4.7 summary + raw)
  • Wireframe v1+ with AI changelog
  • Ronel's $/bottle cost panel
  • Malik's written recommendation
  • Comments thread on the product
Who
  • Joel Binding · Margin / capital
  • Chesky Binding · Strategic fit
  • Malik Advisory · Recommendation + voice
What happens
  • Usually a joint call — Joel + Chesky align in the comments thread
  • Each binding approver: Approve, Request changes, or Reject
  • Malik's "thumbs up / down + reason" appears at the top — strong voice, no veto
  • Mobile-friendly — approvable from phone
Outputs
  • Both binding approvals stamped + dated
  • Or — kill with category + reason
  • Or — sent back to Malik for revisions (sheet edit + re-parse)
Gate to advance Joel AND Chesky = ✓. Auto-advances to PDS + Sampling. The parsed NA is piped to the external Claude PDS project.

  • Pre-loaded PDS instructions (in the external Claude project)
  • Manufacturer auto-locked by type

Who
  • Claude PDS Generates initial formula / concept

Live on Amazon. Thesis vs. actual outcome tracked.
Window 90-day post-launch review
Artifact Live tab — outcome card
Inputs
  • Niche thesis (sales target, BSR target)
  • Actual sales feed
  • Reviews + customer signals

  • April Listing perf + reviews
  • Joel Margin reality check

Amazon-check

Lead · April · ~12h verdict
01
Soft-list to test April lists the product (staged or low-key) to surface Amazon's automated category response.
02
Read the verdict (within 12h) One of three outcomes lands and routes the product:
✓
Allowed — clean No verification required. Production can proceed.
⚠
Allowed · GMP verification required Mfg must provide GMP cert. Adds a few days; usually no extra cost.
⚠
Allowed · product testing required Independent lab testing needed. More expensive — Joel + Chesky decide if it's worth it before Production.
🛑
Hard pull — not allowed Amazon won't list it. Product routes to Killed (or escalated to a different channel).

§ 04

Off-ramps & loops

Not every idea makes it through. Here's where products exit, pause, or loop back.

Killed

From: Idea · Niche Review · PDS · Amazon-check

Hard no. Reason captured (margin, market saturation, regulatory, supply, Amazon hard pull). Stays searchable in killed bucket so the team can spot patterns and not re-pitch the same dead idea in 6 months.

On Hold

From: Any active stage

Soft pause. Waiting on something external — supply availability, a competitor move, a brand decision. Surfaces in the rail with a "revisit on" date. Auto-pings owner when that hits.

Back to Malik

From: Niche Review

"Request changes" sends it back to Malik with specific notes. Doesn't burn the product — keeps the same ID, comments, history. Sheet edit + re-parse, then back through Cost Pass.

Amazon Verdict Pause

From: Build · Amazon-check

Allowed-with-conditions outcomes (GMP cert, lab testing) pause Build until Joel + Chesky decide if the added cost is worth it. Hard-pull verdicts route to Killed.
